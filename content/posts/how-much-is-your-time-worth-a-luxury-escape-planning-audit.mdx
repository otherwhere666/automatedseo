## The hidden cost of DIY travel planning

Let's audit a typical luxury escape planning session. You want five nights somewhere spectacular—maybe Soneva Jani in the Maldives, maybe the Four Seasons Kyoto, maybe Sublime Comporta that's been flooding your LinkedIn feed.

**Hour 1:** Browsing destinations on Instagram and travel blogs. You screenshot seventeen properties, bookmark forty-three articles, and somehow end up reading reviews for La Mamounia in Marrakech that wasn't even on your shortlist.

**Hour 2-3:** Flight comparison hell. Kayak shows $3,200, Google Flights displays $2,800, Singapore Airlines direct quotes $3,600. You discover that the "cheapest" fare doesn't include seat selection, comes with a 14-hour layover in Istanbul, and disappears when you try to book it.

**Hour 4-5:** Hotel research paralysis. Four browser windows, each with twelve tabs open. You're cross-referencing TripAdvisor reviews from 2019 with current Instagram posts, trying to decode whether the Ritz-Carlton Kyoto's "garden view" rooms actually overlook the temple gardens or the service entrance.

> "The average high-net-worth individual spends 6-8 hours planning a single luxury vacation—time that could generate $1,800 to $2,400 in billable revenue."

**Hour 6:** Decision fatigue sets in. You book the Park Hyatt over the Aman because their website loads faster and you're exhausted. Tomorrow is Monday. The confirmation email arrives seventeen minutes later. The club-level room you wanted is no longer available.

Total time invested: Six hours. Opportunity cost at $300/hour: $1,800. Satisfaction level: Mediocre at best.
