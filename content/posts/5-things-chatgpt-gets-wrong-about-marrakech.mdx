## Restaurant advice that ignores the post-pandemic reality

Ask ChatGPT about dining in Marrakech and you'll get a predictable list: Nomad, Le Jardin, Al Fassia. All fine restaurants, but this advice completely misses how COVID reshuffled the city's culinary landscape.

Nomad changed ownership in 2022 and shifted from modern Moroccan cuisine to a more tourist-friendly international menu. Le Jardin remains lovely but now charges 280 MAD for their set menu—pricing that reflects its evolution into primarily a tourist destination rather than the local favorite it once was. Meanwhile, the AI misses entirely on newer standouts like Baromètre, which requires reservations 2+ weeks in advance and has become the city's most coveted table at 450 MAD per person, or the fact that Amal Women's Training Center evolved from a social project into one of Marrakech's most meaningful dining experiences at 280 MAD per person.

The street food recommendations are even more problematic. ## Transportation logistics from a different era

ChatGPT's advice about getting around Marrakech reads like it's from 2020. It'll tell you to negotiate with petit taxis or use Careem—technically accurate but missing crucial context about how transportation actually works in the city now.

Yes, petit taxis exist and officially charge 7-10 MAD for short medina trips, but the fleet is aging and many drivers now prefer longer airport runs (70-100 MAD) over short medina trips. Careem operates in Marrakech, but coverage is spotty compared to Casablanca, and surge pricing during peak tourist season can make a 10-minute ride cost 60+ MAD instead of the standard 25-40 MAD.

The real transportation strategy in 2024? Walking the medina (Bahia Palace to Ben Youssef Medersa is only 1.2km), strategic use of caleches for the 15 MAD route between Koutoubia and Majorelle Gardens, and building relationships with reliable drivers through your riad. Airport transfers work best via pre-arranged hotel pickup (150-200 MAD) rather than hoping for available taxis.

> "AI gives you transportation theory. You need someone who knows that the petit taxi stand near Bahia Palace always has the longest waits between 2-4 PM when drivers break for lunch."

## Seasonal advice that ignores climate reality

ChatGPT will cheerfully recommend visiting Marrakech in April or October without acknowledging that these months have become significantly more unpredictable due to changing weather patterns in North Africa.

April 2023 saw unusual rainfall that flooded parts of the medina, closing Saadian Tombs for three days. October 2024 experienced a heat wave reaching 35°C that made afternoon exploration of Bahia Palace genuinely unpleasant. Meanwhile, January and February—traditionally considered "cool" months—now regularly hit 25°C, perfect for walking the ramparts or exploring Majorelle Gardens.

The seasonal advice also ignores event calendars. ChatGPT doesn't know that the Marrakech Biennale (February 2024) doubles luxury hotel rates, or that Ramadan timing affects Bahia Palace hours (reduced to 10am-4pm) and means most restaurants close until sunset. In 2024, Ramadan runs March 10-April 9, information that dramatically impacts trip planning.

## Shopping guidance that's completely generic

The shopping advice ChatGPT provides for Marrakech could apply to literally any North African medina: negotiate prices, buy rugs, visit the souks. It's the kind of generic guidance that leads to tourist-trap purchases and missed opportunities.

Here's what ChatGPT won't tell you: the best leather goods aren't in the main leather souk anymore—they're at Maroquinerie Place Vendome in Gueliz where Babouche Beldi crafts custom slippers for 280 MAD and you can watch the process. The most interesting textiles come from Cooperative Tissage Tamsift near the Mellah, where hand-woven rugs start at 800 MAD, not from medina vendors selling machine-made "Berber" rugs from Fez at inflated 2000+ MAD prices.

For authentic pottery, skip the main ceramics souk and visit Pottery Salé, where fourth-generation craftsman Mohammed Salé creates custom tagines for 150-300 MAD. His workshop in the Sidi Ghanem industrial district operates Tuesday-Saturday and offers shipping services.

The AI also can't guide you on practical shopping logistics. It won't mention that cooperatives like Tamsift now accept Visa and Mastercard, or that DHL shipping from Marrakech to Europe costs 25 EUR per kilo and typically takes 4-5 days—information that changes how you approach purchasing a 3kg handwoven rug.

> "Generic shopping advice gets you generic tourist purchases. The real finds require knowing that Mohammed Salé's pottery workshop in Sidi Ghanem offers custom tagines you can't find in the medina souks."
